Who are Inanna, Astarte & Isis?

Inanna, Astarte, and Isis are revered divine feminine figures from ancient civilizations. Inanna emerged from Mesopotamia as a goddess associated with love, power, and transformation. Astarte was honored in the Levant and linked with fertility, sovereignty, and celestial symbolism. Isis became one of the most beloved goddesses of ancient Egypt, associated with wisdom, healing, magic, and devotion.

Across history, cultures sometimes blended or connected goddesses through a process called syncretism. Some spiritual traditions see shared themes between them—divine femininity, protection, love, and transformation—while historians generally view them as distinct deities shaped by different cultures.
